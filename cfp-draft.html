Call for Papers

The Unified Search and Recommendation Workshop (USRW 2026) invites submissions exploring the convergence of Information Retrieval (IR) and Recommender Systems (RecSys). Large Language Models, generative retrieval, and agentic architectures are collapsing the historical divide between query-driven search and interaction-driven recommendation. This workshop provides a dedicated venue to accelerate that unification.

USRW is a full-day workshop co-located with the 20th ACM Conference on Recommender Systems (RecSys 2026), taking place on October 2, 2026 in Minneapolis, Minnesota, USA.

Topics of Interest

We welcome papers on models, systems, data, and evaluation at the intersection of IR and RecSys. Topics include but are not limited to:

  • Unified models for search and recommendation (joint objectives, shared item/user representations, cross-task transfer)
  • Sequential, cross-scenario user modelling (co-encoding queries and interactions; intent/session discovery across search and feed)
  • “Search as recommendation” and “recommendation as search” (blending exact matches with collaborative signals; slates grouped by intent)
  • Generative retrieval and Semantic IDs for unified discovery (tokenising items, seq2seq ranking, shared architectures)
  • LLMs for query understanding, intent elicitation, and query synthesis (synthetic queries, autocomplete, document expansion)
  • Agentic discovery assistants (LLM routers/planners that decide when to search vs. recommend; multi-step discovery)
  • Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) for recommendation and search
  • Explainability and results organisation for mixed IR/RecSys slates
  • Data, indexing, and IDs for unified stacks (shared catalogs and logs; semantic identifiers bridging text, behaviour, and media)
  • Evaluation across paradigms (bridging Cranfield-style IR eval with RecSys A/B tests; unified metrics; generalisation and cold-start stress tests)
  • Efficiency and latency for instant, blended experiences
  • Robustness, bias, and safety in unified discovery
  • Industrial case studies and lessons learned
  • Community resources (shared benchmarks/datasets for joint IR-Rec tasks; reproducible baselines and open challenges)

Submission Guidelines

Format: 4–9 pages (excluding references) in ACM SIGCONF double-column format.
Review: Double-blind peer review by at least three reviewers.
Submission system: EasyChair (link TBA — provided by conference organisers).

Authors should use the ACM Primary Article Template (sigconf format). Submissions must be anonymised for double-blind review.

All accepted papers will be presented at the workshop (oral or poster, selected by the programme committee). A Best Paper Award will recognise outstanding contributions.

Workshop authors are expected to present their papers in-person at the conference in Minneapolis.
